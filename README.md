# Supermarket Sales Performance Analysis

## Project Overview
This project analyzes supermarket sales performance across three branches (A, B, and C) during January-March 2019 using Power BI. The objective is to identify sales trends, customer behavior, product performance, and provide actionable business recommendations through data visualization.

## Dataset
- Supermarket Sales Dataset (Public Dataset)
- 1,000 transaction records
- Period: January-March 2019
- Branches: A, B, and C

### Key Variables
- Branch
- Product Line
- Total Sales
- Quantity
- Payment Method
- Rating

## Tools Used
- Power BI
- Microsoft Excel
- Data Visualization
- Business Analysis

## Key Insight

### 1. Sales Declined After January
- January Sales: 339M
- Februari Sales: 269M
- March Sales: 269M

Sales decreased by approximately 20.6% from January to February and remained stable in March.

### 2. Branch C Generated the Highest Revenue
- Branch C: 304M
- Branch B: 292M
- Branch A: 281M

Branch C contributed approximately 34.6% of total sales and became the top-performing branch.

### 3. Revenue Distribution Was Balanced
The revenue gap between branches was relatively small, indicating balanced sales performance across locations.

### 4. Health & Beauty Was the Best-Selling Product Category
Health & Beauty generated the highest sales revenue among all product categories.

### 5. Customer Activity Peaked During Evening Hours
Sales activity reached its highest point around 7 PM, indicating a clear peak shopping period.

### 6. Payment Methods Were Well Distributed
- Cash: 311M
- E-Wallet: 296M
- Credit Card: 270M

Customers showed diverse payment preferences with no dominant payment method.  

## Business Recommendations

- Investigate factors contributing to the sales decline after January.
- Increase promotional activities for Health & Beauty products.
- Analyze and replicate Branch C's best practices across other branches.
- Optimize staffing and inventory during evening peak hours.
